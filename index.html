Health in Minority Groups

The Bridge run series of talks on health in ethnic minority communities due peculiar circumstances of these groups. Social, economic, and cultural barriers can affect health outcomes. Language differences may limit communication with healthcare providers. Discrimination and bias can reduce the quality of care received. Community-based support can improve health awareness and trust. Targeted policies help address these disparities and promote equity.

BBC
The BBC (British Broadcasting Corporation) is the world's leading public service broadcaster, established in 1922 and based in the UK. Operating under a Royal Charter, it provides impartial television, radio, and online content, funded primarily by a UK household licence fee. The BBC aims to inform, educate, and entertain globally.
Volunteer Scotland
Volunteer Scotland is the national centre for volunteering in Scotland, dedicated to promoting, supporting, and celebrating volunteering. It helps individuals find opportunities, offers guidance and training for organisations, and advocates for policy changes to make volunteering more accessible, inclusive, and impactful across Scotland.
ACVO TSI
ACVO (Aberdeen Council of Voluntary Organisations) TSI is the Third Sector Interface for Aberdeen, acting as the central hub for volunteering, charities, and community groups. It supports local non-profits with governance, funding, and networking, while promoting volunteering opportunities and connecting the voluntary sector with local and national government.

Our Impact

Small Steps
Big Outcomes

“

Linking Lives Aberdeen works to reduce loneliness and social isolation by pairing up volunteers with elderly people who would benefit from a chat. Project coordinator Olushola Ajide has been running the service since last February and knows how difficult the festive period can be – even before coronavirus restricted time with loved ones. He said: “For those receiving phone calls it’s been great. They’re appreciative of the fact they feel helped and less lonely. They look forward to the call – it helps their mental well being.”

The Press and Journal - 26 December 2020
“

Aberdeen Linking Lives, our befriending service that tackles loneliness and social isolation is a finalist at the Celebrate Aberdeen Awards under Community Impact. Since our launch in 2019, we’ve delivered over 19,000 hours of befriending. But what we offer is far more than time, it’s presence, consistency, and care. One service user recently told us, “I really appreciate your call. You’re the first person I’ve spoken to all week.”

Aberdeen Linking Lives's Post - 5 October 2025
